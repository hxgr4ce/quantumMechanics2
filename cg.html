Clebsch-Gordan Coefficients

A state \(|j_1, j_2; J, M\rangle\) of a system with two subsystems can be written as a linear combination of states where \(\sigma_1\) and \(\sigma_2\) are specified. More specifically:

\[\begin{align} |j_1, j_2; J, M\rangle &= \sum_{m_1, m_2}|j_1, m_2\rangle \otimes |j_2, m_2\rangle C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2} \\ &= \sum_{m_1, m_2}|j_1, j_2; m_1, m_2\rangle C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2} \end{align}\] (Where the tensor product \(|j_1, m_2\rangle \otimes |j_2, m_2\rangle\) is sometimes written as \(|j_1, j_2; m_1, m_2\rangle\). This is quite confusing, so I try not to use this notation if I can.)

For example, in the simple example of the addition of angular momenta, we found the following eigenstates of \(\hat J^2\) for \(J=0, 1\): \[\begin{align} &|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, 1\rangle = |++\rangle \\ &|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, 0\rangle = \frac{|+-\rangle + |-+\rangle}{\sqrt 2} \\ &|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, -1\rangle = |--\rangle \\ &|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 0, 0\rangle = \frac{|+-\rangle - |-+\rangle}{\sqrt 2} \end{align}\] by doing an awful lot of algebra. This means that the only state that contributes to \(|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, 1\rangle\) is the state where \(m_1\) (or \(\sigma_1\)) is \(\frac{1}{2}\) and \(m_2\) (or \(\sigma_2\)) is also \(\frac{1}{2}\).

In other words, \[\begin{align} |\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, 1\rangle &= \sum_{\sigma_1, \sigma_2}|\frac{1}{2}, \sigma_2\rangle \otimes |\frac{1}{2}, \sigma_2\rangle C^{11}_{\frac{1}{2},\sigma_1, \frac{1}{2}, \sigma_2} \\ &= |\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\rangle\otimes|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\rangle (1) \\ &= |+\rangle \otimes |+\rangle = |++\rangle \end{align}\] which is exactly what we found. Let's also do this with the \(|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, 0\rangle\) state. We look to the \(\frac{1}{2}\times\frac{1}{2}\) table for the column where \(J=1, M=0\).

This time, there are two rows, one for the state where \(m_1=\frac{1}{2}, m_2=-\frac{1}{2}\), which has coefficient \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}\) and another for the state where \(m_1 = -\frac{1}{2}, m_2=\frac{1}{2}\), which has coefficient \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}\).

ALL coefficients in the above Clebsch-Gordan coefficient table have the square root omitted for space efficiency. For example, all \(\frac{1}{2}\)'s should really be read as \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}\).

So:

\[\begin{align} |\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}; 1, 0\rangle &= \sum_{\sigma_1, \sigma_2}|\frac{1}{2}, \sigma_2\rangle \otimes |\frac{1}{2}, \sigma_2\rangle C^{10}_{\frac{1}{2},\sigma_1, \frac{1}{2}, \sigma_2} \\ &= |\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\rangle\otimes|\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{2}\rangle (\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}) + |\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{2}\rangle\otimes|\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}\rangle (\sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}) \\ &= \sqrt{\frac{1}{2}}(|+-\rangle + |-+\rangle) \end{align}\] which is also exactly what we found.

The Recurrence Relation for the Clebsch-Gordan Coefficients

The Clebsch-Gordan coefficients can be recursively defined like so: \[ C^{J M \pm 1}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2} = \sqrt{\frac{j_1(j_1+1) - m_1(m_1\mp 1)}{J(J+1) - M(M\pm 1)}} C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1\mp 1 j_2, m_2} + \sqrt{\frac{j_2(j_2+1) - m_2(m_2\mp 1)}{J(J+1) - M(M\pm 1)}} C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1 j_2, m_2\mp 1} \]

Properties of the Clebsch-Gordan Coefficients

  • Orthonormality: \[\langle JM|J'M'\rangle = \delta_{JJ'} \delta_{MM'} = \sum_{m_1, m_2}C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2}C^{J'M'}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2}\]
  • Identity: \[\sum_{J=|j_1-j_2|}^{j_1+j_2} \sum_{M=-J}^J |JM \rangle\langle JM| = \hat 1\]
  • More orthonormality: \[\begin{align} \langle m_1 m_2|m_1'm_2'\rangle &= \delta_{m_1 m_1'} \delta_{m_2, m_2'} = \sum_{JM}\langle m_1 m_2|JM \rangle\langle JM|m_1'm_2'\rangle \\ &= \sum_{JM} C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2} C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1', j_2, m_2'} \end{align}\]
  • Not everything needs a title okay: \[|m_1 m_2\rangle = \sum_{JM}|JM\rangle\langle JM|m_1 m_2\rangle = \sum_{JM}|JM\rangle C^{JM}_{j_1, m_1, j_2, m_2}\]
  • \(C^{c\gamma}_{a\alpha b\beta} = 0\) when \(\gamma=\alpha + \beta\)
  • 3-j Coefficients: \[C^{c\gamma}_{a\alpha b\beta} = (-1)^{a - b + \gamma}\sqrt{2c + 1}\pmatrix{a & b & c \\ \alpha & \beta & -\gamma}\] where \((-1)^{a - b + \gamma}\) is a phase factor, \(\sqrt{2c + 1}\) is a normalization factor, and \(\pmatrix{a & b & c \\ \alpha & \beta & \gamma}\) is called a 3-j coefficient, as is incariant under cyclic permutations of its columns.
  • More about 3-j coefficients \(\pmatrix{a & b & c \\ \alpha & \beta & \gamma} = 0\) if \(\gamma = \alpha + \beta\), and \[ \pmatrix{a & b & c \\ \alpha & \beta & \gamma} = \pmatrix{b&c&a \\ \beta & \gamma & \alpha} = \pmatrix{c&a&b \\ \gamma & \alpha & \beta} = (-1)^{a + b + c} \pmatrix{a&c&b \\ \alpha & \gamma & \beta} = \pmatrix{a & b & c \\ -\alpha & -\beta & -\gamma} \]
