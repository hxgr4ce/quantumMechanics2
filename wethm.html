The Wigner-Eckhart Theorem

The Wigner-Eckhart Theorem is as follows: \[ \langle \alpha, JM|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha', J'M'\rangle = C^{JM}_{J'M'k\mu}\frac{\langle \alpha J || \hat T^{(k)} || \alpha'J'\rangle}{\sqrt{2J + 1}} \] Note that on the left hand side of the equation, we have three magnetic quantum numbers, \(M, \mu, M'\) which define how each component is oriented in space. The left hand side of the equation places all of the dependence on magnetic numbers on the Clebsch-Gordan coefficient, and the reduced-matrix element \(\langle \alpha J || \hat T^{(k)} || \alpha'J'\rangle\) is totally independent of magnetic numbers. Also note that on its own, \(\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha', J'M'\rangle\) is not well defined. It is projected onto a specific value with \(\langle \alpha, JM|\).

We can demonstrate the Wigner-Eckhart theorem by showing that \(\langle \alpha, JM|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha', J'M'\rangle\) follows the same recurrence relations as the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. To do this, we use the commutation relations that we found here.

Say that we have a state \(\langle \alpha, jm|\hat T^{(k)}_k|\alpha', j'j'\rangle\). Here, the magnetic numbers \(M, \mu, M'\) are all as large as possible, given that a magnetic number \(m = -j,\cdots,j\). \(\hat T^{(k)}_k|\alpha', j'j'\rangle\) is also evidently an eigenstate of \(\hat J_z\) where \(m=k+j'\) and \(j_{total}=k+j'\).

Recalling our demonstration of the general case for the addition of angular momenta, we know that \(\langle \alpha, jm|\hat T^{(k)}_k|\alpha', j'j'\rangle\) must be equal to \(\delta_{j, k+j'}\delta_{m, k+j'} = C^{j'+k, j'+k}_{jj'kk}=1\).

Now, if we know \(\langle \alpha, jm|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha', j'm'\rangle\) for \(m=m, m+1,\cdots, j\), then we can do the following: Using the generalized commutation properties of spherical tensors, \[\begin{align} \langle \alpha, jm|[\hat J_+, \hat T^{(k)}_\mu]|\alpha', j'm'\rangle &= \hbar\sqrt{k(k+1)-\mu(\mu+1)}\langle \alpha, jm|\hat T^{(k)}_{\mu+1}|\alpha', j'm'\rangle \end{align}\] The left hand side of this equation is: \[\begin{align} \langle \alpha, jm|[\hat J_+, \hat T^{(k)}_\mu]|\alpha', j'm'\rangle &= \langle\alpha jm|\hat J_+\hat T^{(k)}_{\mu}|\alpha' j'm'\rangle - \langle\alpha jm|\hat T^{(k)}_{\mu}\hat J_+|\alpha' j'm'\rangle \\ &= \hbar\sqrt{j(j+1)-m(m-1)}\langle\alpha jm-1|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'\rangle - \hbar\sqrt{j'(j'+1)-m'(m'+1)}\langle\alpha j m|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'+1\rangle \end{align}\] and setting this equal to the right hand side of the equation yields: \[\begin{align} \hbar\sqrt{j(j+1)-m(m-1)}\langle\alpha jm-1|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'\rangle &= \hbar\sqrt{j'(j'+1)-m'(m'+1)}\langle\alpha j m|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'+1\rangle + \hbar\sqrt{k(k+1)-\mu(\mu+1)}\langle \alpha, jm|\hat T^{(k)}_{\mu+1}|\alpha', j'm'\rangle \\ \langle\alpha jm-1|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'\rangle &= \sqrt{\frac{j'(j'+1)-m'(m'+1)}{j(j+1)-m(m-1)}}\langle\alpha j m|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'+1\rangle + \sqrt{\frac{k(k+1)-\mu(\mu+1)}{j(j+1)-m(m-1)}}\langle \alpha, jm|\hat T^{(k)}_{\mu+1}|\alpha', j'm'\rangle \end{align}\] Clearly, \(\langle\alpha jm-1|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'\rangle\) follows the exact same recurrence relation as the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients! Hence we see that indeed, \(\langle\alpha jm-1|\hat T^{(k)}_\mu|\alpha' j'm'\rangle \propto C^{jm}_{j'm'k\mu}\), just as the Wigner-Eckhart theorem states.
