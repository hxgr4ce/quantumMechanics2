Wigner D-Matrices

Wigner D-Matrices were introduced here. We have \((2j+1)\)-dimensional vector space \(V_{kj} = \text{span}\{|kjm\rangle, m = -j, -j+1,\cdots, j-1, j\}\) which is invariant under rotations. An arbitrary vector in this space, \(|\psi\rangle\) is rotated like so: \[\begin{align} |\psi\rangle &= \sum_{m = -j}^j|kjm\rangle a_m \\ \hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)|\psi\rangle &= \hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)\sum_{m = -j}^j|kjm\rangle a_m \\ &= \sum_{m = -j}^j\hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)|kjm\rangle a_m \\ &= \sum_{m,m' = -j}^j |kjm'\rangle\langle kjm'| \hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)|kjm\rangle a_m \\ &= \sum_{m, m' = -j}^j |kjm'\rangle D^j_{m'm}(\hat R) a_m \end{align}\] The expression for \( D^j_{m'm}(\hat R) = \langle kjm'| \hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)|kjm\rangle\) can be reduced slightly by representing \(\hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)\) as an Euler rotation instead. \[\begin{align} D^j_{m'm}(\hat R) &= \langle kjm'| \hat R_{\hat n}(\phi)|kjm\rangle \\ &= \langle kjm'| \hat R(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)|kjm\rangle \\ &= \langle kjm'| \hat R_{\hat z}(\alpha) \hat R_{\hat y}(\beta) \hat R_{\hat z}(\gamma)|kjm\rangle \\ &= e^{-i(\alpha m'+\beta m)} \langle kjm'| \hat R_{\hat y}(\beta) |kjm\rangle \\ &= e^{-i(\alpha m'+\beta m)} \langle kjm'| e^{-i\beta\hat J_y/\hbar} |kjm\rangle \\ &= e^{-i(\alpha m'+\beta m)} d^j_{m'm}(\beta) \\ \end{align}\] This \(D^j_{m'm}(\alpha, \beta,\gamma)\) is the Wigner d-matrix and \(d^j_{m'm}(\beta)\) is the reduced Wigner d-matrix (note that you can also say \(D^j_{m'm}(\alpha, \beta,\gamma)\) instead of \(D^j_{m'm}(\hat R)\) ). Typically, you use pre-tabulated results when working with both, as they are very complicated to computer, especially for the higher dimensional matrices.
